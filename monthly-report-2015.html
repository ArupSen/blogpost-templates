One other thing that has carried over after writing the report from September 2015 is that because all of these goals are ongoing there isn't a specific end point apart from the Audax goal that I have set for this year which has 3 months left to run. If the other goals had clearer and more defined end points then it would be easier to create monthly sub-goals in order to move towards them more easily. What I've been doing with these reports is simply to account for what has been happening and hopefully to write about it as soon as it happens to keep things as fresh as possible but haven't thought of it as a tool for formulating goals. It does sort of happen by accident but last month after crunching the numbers for a healthier eating plan and coming up with one portion of fruit and veg per meal, I thought I could do a similar thing to my other goals. Sometimes even though a clear end point isn't apparent, if I had to sit down and describe it to someone then a better idea would emerge and it is something that I need to do with each of the goals listed below.   • Healthier diet

    As I've mentioned already in the introduction that I have a goal for this month which is add one portion of fruit or veg to each of my meals. This is for the five days on which I eat my normal amount of calories. It's easy to remember in that you simply have to add one portion every meal. I also eat a lot of bread, especially toast and it makes up a good portion of my diet. When I'm hungry and I don't want to cook, I end up eating a lot of toast. Watching a few healthy eating videos on YouTube I noticed that they mentioned they were trying to cut down or even to eliminate bread from their diet. This wouldn't work for me as I love bread so much but I'm going to try and eat porridge instead of toast along with my new regime of adding scrambled eggs to my breakfast. I'm not planning to do this for each and every breakfast but perhaps for one or two meals just to see how it goes. Obviously I'll report back here. The other idea that I came across with healthy eating was batch cooking. It's something that I've done when my daughter was just coming up to her first year but hadn't thought of doing it longer term. But it struck a chord because one of the main reasons that I end up eating junk food is that when it is time to eat I don't have anything healthy prepared. I often do have the ingredients to prepare a healthy meal but don't want to spend the next 45 minutes to an hour cooking and preparing that meal. I don't enjoy cooking as much as I used to. Batch cooking would make perfect sense on both those counts. I could cook enough for the week and then if I did want to cook or eat something else I would have that option because the meal would be frozen but then if I was in a hurry or just not in the mood I could pop a ready prepared meal into the microwave.

    That makes quite a few new ideas to try out this month: extra protein, batch cooking and one portion of fruit or veg per meal on eating days. Oh, and porridge.
